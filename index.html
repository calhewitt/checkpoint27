Our history

Ever since its foundation by the 4th Viscount Earley in 1786, Checkpoint 27 has remained the highpoint of the sponsored walk, delighting ramblers across the centuries with its idyllic woodland vistas and universally acclaimed mud. Originally attracted by the opportunity for birdwatching nearby, (despite years of human habitation, at the clearest times the lesser spotted ceagle can still occasionally be glimpsed amongst the trees) the Earley family soon realised the value of their acquisition, and devoted years to developing the checkpoint into the world-renowned landmark that we know today. Maintaining a unique balance between the peaceful ambience of the countryside and the proximity of the bustling metropolis of the halfway point, Checkpoint 27 is considered a second home to the thousands of appreciative visitors who continue to flock from across the globe every year.
